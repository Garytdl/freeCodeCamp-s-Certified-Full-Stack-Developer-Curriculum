      <p>Finger strength is crucial for climbing because your grip supports your entire body on holds of all sizes. To build it, climbers use hangboards, campus boards, and grip trainers, gradually increasing intensity while avoiding overstrain. Strong fingers improve your ability to hold small edges, pinch grips, and slopers, allowing you to tackle harder routes and climb longer without fatigue.       <p>Core tension is essential in climbing because it stabilizes your body, keeps your movements controlled, and allows you to efficiently transfer power from your legs to your arms. Exercises like planks, leg raises, and hollow body holds strengthen core muscles, improving overall climbing efficiency and making even difficult routes feel more manageable.</p>
